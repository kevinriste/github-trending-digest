Terence Tao, at 8 years old (1984) [pdf]

gwern.net  ·  480 pts  ·  326 comments  ·  discussion

Analysis

This 1984 academic paper by M.A. (Ken) Clements provides a detailed biographical and pedagogical assessment of a seven-year-old Terence Tao. The study documents Tao’s precocious mathematical abilities, which were discovered after a newspaper article highlighted his high school-level performance while still in primary school. Through a series of tests, observations, and interviews, the author catalogs Tao’s mastery of advanced calculus, linear algebra, and computer programming, alongside his preference for analytic, non-visual problem-solving methods over purely intuitive or geometric strategies.

No 08  ·  Caution Tape  ·  Security Alert

Show HN: enveil – hide your .env secrets from prAIng eyes

github.com  ·  200 pts  ·  130 comments  ·  discussion

Analysis

`Enject` (formerly known as `enveil`) is a Rust-based utility designed to protect `.env` file secrets from being exposed to AI coding agents and LLM tools. Instead of storing plaintext credentials on disk, it uses an encrypted local store to hold secret values, while the `.env` file contains only symbolic `en://` references. When a user runs a command, the tool prompts for a master password to decrypt the secrets in memory and injects them directly into the subprocess at launch, ensuring plaintext sensitive data is never written to the filesystem.
