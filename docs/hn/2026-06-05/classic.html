While previous models successfully demonstrated how entanglement builds the static structure of space, they failed to explain how matter causes space-time to curve. Recent research by Charles Cao and his collaborators suggests that "magic"—a specific type of quantum complexity generated by non-Clifford gates in quantum error-correcting codes—provides the necessary flexibility for space-time to interact with matter. This discovery identifies magic as the potential mechanism behind gravity, indicating that gravitational effects arise from the imperfect, mixed encoding of quantum information. This theoretical progress is significant because it provides a foundational framework for simulating quantum gravity and suggests that the fundamental properties of our universe are direct manifestations of quantum mechanics.
