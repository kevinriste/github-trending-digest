Security Alert

VS Code Vulnerability Allows One-Click GitHub Token Theft

blog.ammaraskar.com  ·  440 pts  ·  65 comments

A critical flaw in the platform's webview event-handling allows malicious links to simulate keystrokes and exfiltrate OAuth credentials. Developers are urged to restrict extension permissions and move toward short-lived, repository-specific tokens to mitigate the risk.

Read →Share →
[ Analysis + ]

Technical Analysis

Researcher Ammar Askar has disclosed a critical security vulnerability in VS Code and github.dev that allows attackers to exfiltrate GitHub OAuth tokens via a single malicious link. The exploit functions by leveraging VS Code’s webview "keydown" event-handling mechanism to simulate user keystrokes, which can then be used to install malicious extensions or execute unauthorized commands. Because github.dev uses long-lived OAuth tokens with broad access, this flaw poses a significant risk to the security of both public and private repositories. Askar chose full public disclosure due to past frustrations with Microsoft’s Security Response Center (MSRC), highlighting an ongoing tension between security researchers and the company's bug reporting policies.

Reader Reactions

The core consensus is that VS Code’s extension model is fundamentally insecure, as extensions act as full-privileged Node.js applications capable of silent token exfiltration and unauthorized file system access.

While many commenters advocate for switching to Neovim for better security and transparency, others note that Neovim itself can be unstable and requires significant time investment to overcome habit-based resistance.

Developers should mitigate risks by using isolated extension profiles, strictly controlling plugin permissions, and moving toward short-lived, repository-specific tokens to limit the blast radius if an environment becomes compromised.

Repurposing Nvidia GPU VRAM as Linux Swap Space

github.com  ·  330 pts  ·  88 comments

By intercepting the CUDA API, the nbd-vram project creates a high-priority swap partition for memory-constrained systems. While sequential throughput faces limitations, the approach effectively trades NBD overhead for superior random access latency compared to standard storage.

Read →Share →
[ Analysis + ]

Technical Analysis

The `nbd-vram` project allows Linux users to repurpose an idle Nvidia GPU's VRAM as a high-priority swap device, providing a practical memory expansion solution for laptops with limited or soldered RAM. By utilizing a userspace daemon and the NBD (Network Block Device) protocol to interface with the CUDA API, the tool avoids the hardware-level restrictions that typically prevent consumer GPUs from mapping VRAM directly to the kernel. While the implementation introduces some overhead that impacts peak sequential throughput, it significantly outperforms NVMe drives in random access latency by avoiding power-state transitions. This approach offers a reliable, low-latency alternative for managing sporadic memory pressure without requiring kernel-level modifications or proprietary driver access.

Reader Reactions

Participants generally agree that while the concept of using VRAM as Linux swap is a fun technical experiment, the current implementation is too inefficient for real-world performance gains or production utility.

Critics argue that modern NVMe storage is already fast enough for most swapping needs and that the architectural overhead of NBD and userspace bounce buffers negates the speed benefits of VRAM.

Developers suggest that achieving usable bandwidth would require moving away from NBD toward custom block drivers, while noting that true memory expansion is currently gated by PCIe and cache coherency limitations.

AI Responses Outperform Human Professors in Legal Reasoning Trials

law.stanford.edu  ·  265 pts  ·  206 comments

Blind evaluations at Stanford Law School reveal that experts favor AI-generated answers to contract law inquiries. The study marks a significant shift in pedagogical discourse, suggesting that large language models are reaching professional parity in nuanced judgment tasks.

Read →Share →
[ Analysis + ]

Technical Analysis

A recent study conducted by researchers at Stanford Law School found that law professors preferred AI-generated responses to student questions over those written by their peers in 75% of head-to-head, blind evaluations. By testing contract law inquiries that required nuanced legal reasoning rather than simple factual recall, the researchers demonstrated that large language models could consistently meet professional standards for pedagogical quality. This finding is significant because it challenges long-standing assumptions about AI’s inability to handle tasks requiring complex judgment and suggests that these tools could serve as effective, on-demand tutors in rigorous academic environments. While the authors caution against the immediate, wholesale adoption of these systems, the study highlights a necessary shift in the legal education discourse from debating AI's fundamental competence to determining how to implement such technologies responsibly.

Reader Reactions

Commenters express skepticism toward the study's statistical validity and methodology, suggesting the AI's performance might reflect its ability to produce superficially pleasing, academic-style answers rather than accurate legal reasoning.

Some participants argue that LLMs act as invaluable research assistants for identifying niche cases and brainstorming counter-arguments, provided that humans maintain rigorous oversight to catch potentially dangerous, hallucinated legal citations.

Professionals note that legal documents lack the automated testing, sandboxing, and rapid debugging cycles found in software development, making the high-stakes risk of AI-generated errors significantly harder to detect and mitigate.

Molecular Research VIII research.ligo.bio

The Surprising Structural Redundancy of Natural Protein Folds

research.ligo.bio  ·  100 pts  ·  28 comments

Scaling sequence data may yield diminishing returns in structural diversity, as nature heavily prioritizes the reuse of established scaffolds.

Spectral bisection analysis indicates that biological structural space is far more concentrated than previously assumed.

Read →Share →
[ Analysis + ]

Technical Analysis

Arda Goreci’s analysis of protein fold redundancy challenges the common assumption that scaling genomic and metagenomic sequence data necessarily yields proportional increases in structural diversity for training generative biomolecular models. By utilizing spectral bisection and rigorous structural clustering, the Ligo team discovered that natural protein space is surprisingly concentrated, with the vast majority of structural diversity contained within a relatively small number of fold neighborhoods. This finding suggests that simply scaling data may be insufficient for achieving true innovation in enzyme design, as evolution heavily favors the reuse of established scaffolds over the invention of entirely novel ones. Consequently, future model development may need to balance the natural frequency of these common folds with a more intentional sampling strategy to effectively explore the limits of the protein manifold.

Reader Reactions

Commenters generally agree that the limited diversity of natural protein folds is a byproduct of evolution, which prioritizes robust, energetically favorable, and familiar structures over exploring the entire theoretical sequence space.

A central point of contention is whether nature’s restricted fold repertoire reflects an inherent physical limitation or simply the conservative "tinkering" process of evolution that biases biological systems toward reusing existing patterns.

Protein structures are highly tolerant to mutation, with many sequences folding into identical shapes, suggesting that engineers could potentially discover vast, untapped fold spaces by designing proteins from first principles.

CT Scans Reveal the Efficiency of BYD Component Integration

lumafield.com  ·  394 pts  ·  237 comments

Computed tomography of BYD vehicle parts highlights the manufacturer's total vertical integration strategy. By internalizing the production of battery cells and control systems, the company has established a new benchmark for cost efficiency in electric vehicle manufacturing.

Technical Analysis

This article presents a detailed technical analysis of various BYD vehicle components, including battery cells, a window switch panel, a portable charger, and a key fob, using computed tomography (CT) to reveal their internal design and construction. While the components themselves represent standard engineering practices, the analysis highlights BYD’s profound vertical integration, which allows the company to design and manufacture nearly every part of the vehicle in-house. Engineering analysis suggests that modern vehicle reliability is defined by advanced "E-axle" designs and high-quality electrical connectors, which have significantly improved over the last few decades, rendering traditional mechanical concerns largely obsolete.
